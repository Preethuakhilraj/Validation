Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix

Author: J. K. Rowling

Published date: 20004

Status: Available in Library

"Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix" is the fifth book in J.K. Rowling's immensely popular Harry Potter series. Published in 2003, it continues the story of Harry Potter, a young wizard attending Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. The book begins with Harry spending a difficult summer at the Dursleys' home, where he is isolated and cut off from the wizarding world. Upon returning to Hogwarts for his fifth year, Harry discovers that few people believe his account of Voldemort's return, leading to tensions and divisions within the magical community. Throughout the school year, Harry faces various challenges, including the oppressive atmosphere created by the new Defense Against the Dark Arts teacher, Dolores Umbridge, who is appointed by the Ministry of Magic. Harry and his friends also form Dumbledore's Army, a secret group to learn and practice defensive magic. As the story unfolds, Harry learns more about Voldemort's plans, uncovers secrets about his own past, and faces heartbreaking losses. The book builds toward a climactic battle at the Ministry of Magic, where Harry and his allies confront Death Eaters and uncover crucial information about Voldemort's quest for power. "Order of the Phoenix" is known for its darker tone compared to earlier books in the series, as well as its exploration of themes such as rebellion, loss, and the consequences of war. It is a pivotal installment in the Harry Potter series, setting the stage for the events that follow in the final books.
