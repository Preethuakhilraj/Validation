True North

Author: Jim Harrison

Published: 2004

Status: Available in Library


Borrow Add to wishlist

"True North" is a novel by Jim Harrison, known for his rich storytelling and vivid characters. Published in 2004, it follows the life of David Burkett, a Michigan-based real estate developer dealing with family drama, business challenges, and personal struggles. The novel delves into themes of love, ambition, betrayal, and redemption, set against the backdrop of the rugged northern Michigan landscape. Harrison's writing style is often praised for its lyrical prose and deep exploration of human nature, making "True North" a compelling read for fans of literary fiction.

The scion of a family of wealthy timber barons, David Burkett has grown up with a father who is a malevolent force, and a mother made vague and numb by alcohol and pills. He and his sister Cynthia, a firecracker who scandalizes the family at sixteen by taking up with the son of their Finnish-Native American gardener, are mostly left to make their own way. As David comes to adulthood, he realizes he must come to terms with his forefathers' rapacious destruction of the woods of Michigan's Upper Peninsula, as well as with the working people who made their wealth possible.
