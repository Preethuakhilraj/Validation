The Secret Language of Birthdays

Author: Gary Goldschneider& Joost Elffers

Published date: 11/11/2001

Status: Available in Library


Borrow Add to wishlist

"The Secret Language of Birthdays" is a book written by Gary Goldschneider and Joost Elffers. It delves into the idea that the day you were born can reveal unique personality traits and tendencies. Here's an overview of the book's content and its impact: Concept: The book introduces the concept that each day of the year is associated with specific characteristics, strengths, weaknesses, and life paths. It's based on astrology and numerology, blending these ideas to create a detailed personality profile for each birthdate. Structure: The book is structured as a reference guide, with each day of the year corresponding to a specific personality profile. Readers can look up their birthdate to explore the traits associated with it. Personality Profiles: Each birthday entry includes information such as strengths, weaknesses, career tendencies, relationship dynamics, and overall life themes. It offers insights into how individuals born on that day might approach various aspects of life. Impact: "The Secret Language of Birthdays" has gained a significant following since its publication. Many readers find it intriguing and entertaining, even if they don't take astrology or numerology too seriously. It's often used as a fun way to explore personality traits and understand oneself or others better. Criticism: While the book has its fans, it also faces criticism from skeptics who question the scientific validity of using birthdates to determine personality traits. Some argue that the descriptions are too generalized and could apply to many people regardless of their birthdate. Overall, the book has become a popular cultural phenomenon, sparking conversations about astrology, personality typing, and the ways in which people perceive themselves and others based on when they were born.
